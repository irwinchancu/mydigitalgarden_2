A career guide is a plan highlighting what it will take to advance your career in ways that you find meaningful. It includes four parts, each meant to challenge you to think critically about what you want and where you should focus your energy. The goal is to identify and take steps that will help you align your career with your deeper purpose and skills.

## Part One: Recognize Your Career Traps

Career traps are familiar patterns of thinking or behaviors that we practice even though they can negatively impact us. They fall into five buckets: ambition, expectation, busyness, translation, and adrenaline.

Read through the following career traps, and check the ones you’ve ever fallen into:

  **Ambition trap:** You’re a high performer used to success. You worry that if you slow down, you’ll stop achieving. Your solution is to work harder when pressure rises at work.

  **Expectation trap:** You strive to meet the expectations of others. Admitting you’re struggling is ego shattering. You worry people will think less of you if you acknowledge you’re burned out.

  **Busyness trap:** You like being busy, and work always comes first. As a result, you struggle to say no, to slow down, or to switch off. You sacrifice time with loved ones for your job.

  **Translation trap:** You’ve worked hard to get where you are, but happiness still eludes you. You feel like you’ve lost your way because your role doesn’t fulfill or inspire you. You believe your current job is all you know.

  **Adrenaline trap:** You run your life on adrenaline, not taking enough time to care for your body or mind. You forget that putting your self-care needs first is critical for a sustainable career.

Avoiding career traps involves making trade-offs, and deciding on those trade-offs is easier when you’re clear on what matters to you.
