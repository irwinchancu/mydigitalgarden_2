# Build an Idea Résumé

To persuade a higher-up that one of your ideas is worth pursuing, empower yourself (and your ideas) with a strong, data-driven pitch that shows strategic value, or what is called “an idea résumé.”

An idea résumé represents a clear, structured way that you, as a team member, can add value to your organization and gives you a better shot at sparking change. It’s a document that shares your initial thoughts in a clear, concise, and controlled way, while explicitly connecting your idea to the broader goals of the company, as well as to the pain points you’re trying to soothe.

## Outline the Problem

Begin by documenting the problem that catalyzed your idea, outlining the issue, its potential causes, its practical effects on the organization, and any data you can gather as evidence to support these points.

## Pitch Your Idea

Your idea must provide some sort of value for both the company and its people, so showcase how it will benefit everyone involved. Remember that you have the most direct, experiential knowledge of your business and its customers. Use what you know to build an idea résumé your boss can’t ignore.
