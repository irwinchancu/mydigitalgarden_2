You can ask gen AI to take the role of a specialist in listening skills, self-awareness, and emotional intelligence and engage in a conversation with you. While this is a typical one-on-one setting in which you have a conversation with AI, the feedback collection process itself opens up to a group setting (one-to-many), where you seek input from team members, peers, and stakeholders.

**Co-Thinker’s role.** Gen AI acts as an expert coach, helping managers improve their ability to ask for candid feedback.

**Setting.** The setting for the dialogue is a one-on-one interaction, where the manager engages with gen AI as a Co-Thinker in a private environment, ideal for introspection and personalized guidance.

## Speech Preparation

|   |
|---|
|READ MORE<br><br>HBR Guide to Generative AI for Managers<br><br>Chapter 9<br><br>Gen AI for Persuasive Communication<br><br>Pages 85–86|

Using gen AI as a Co-Thinker can help you become a more effective communicator: preparing your speech for an important event, defining both the “what” of your speech (defining the storytelling, sharpening the messages and identifying potential weak points, preempting objections, leveraging metaphors and examples) as well as the “how” (setting the tone, the rhythm, eye contact, posture, gestures).

**Co-Thinker’s role.** Gen AI acts as a communication expert, applying the best practices for persuasive speeches.

**Setting.** The setting for the dialogue is one-on-one as the manager interacts with the machine in preparing the speech. However, at the end of the sequence, gen AI can suggest rehearsing the speech in front of colleagues and asking for their feedback. Based on the input from the rehearsal, the manager can return to the machine dialogue for further revisions. Additionally, the manager can record the rehearsal, upload the file, and ask the machine to provide feedback on the performance.

#### Instructions

|   |
|---|
|You (Gen AI) act as a communication expert. You guide me (manager) as I prepare for a speech.<br><br>I want you (Gen AI) to follow these instructions:<br><br>[Step 1] Gen AI asks the manager to provide contextual input about the topic and the audience. ## Root Cause Analysis

|   |
|---|
|READ MORE<br><br>HBR Guide to Generative AI for Managers<br><br>Chapter 14<br><br>Gen AI for Complex Problem-Solving<br><br>Pages 140–141|

When diagnosing a problem, you can ask gen AI to act as a methodology expert and guide the reflection to identify its root causes. You, your team, and gen AI can explore the problem thoroughly, peeling back the layers to uncover underlying factors and converging on potential solution approaches.

**Co-Thinker’s role.** Gen AI acts as an expert of problem analysis, applying the fishbone approach (also known as the Ishikawa diagram).

**Setting.** The setting for this dialogue is a one-to-many interaction between gen AI and the team, including the manager. This is an example of a team meeting or workshop in which gen AI acts as an expert and actively contributes to the team discussion. Depending on the gen AI model you use, it may also be possible to have a more immersive conversation using a voice-to-voice feature.

|---|

#### Instructions

When you are ready to begin, copy and paste everything within the box below (including the guidelines) into your gen AI model.

|   |
|---|
|You (Gen AI) act as an expert of problem analysis, applying the fishbone approach (also known as the Ishikawa diagram). You guide me (manager) in solving a business problem.<br><br>I want you (Gen AI) to follow these instructions:<br><br>[Step 1] Gen AI asks the manager to describe the problem providing relevant background information. With many industries considering their VUCA environment, almost every strategic initiative faces multiple risks that leaders must address to ensure success. By proactively mapping risks across potential business ventures, leaders can anticipate any vulnerabilities. Generative AI as a Co-Thinker can help you identify and mitigate risk in various ways.

**Co-Thinker’s role.** Gen AI acts as an expert in innovation management, applying frameworks such as Rita McGrath and Ian MacMillan’s “discovery-driven planning,” an approach for de-risking high-uncertainty projects and learning from potential mistakes as inexpensively and as early as possible.

**Setting.** The setting for the dialogue can be one-to-one, with the manager and gen AI engaging in dialogue, or one-to-many, in a group setting where other team members join the manager. This arrangement allows more pauses for group reflection before returning to the dialogue with gen AI in the sequenced conversation.
